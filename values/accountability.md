# Accountability

> Own your part. That's all. Just your part.

## For our group this means

We take responsibility for what we bring into the room. Our words, our presence, our impact on others. We don't blame the group for what we put into it.

This isn't about perfection. We will get it wrong. The accountability is in what happens next: we don't deflect, don't minimise, don't go quiet and hope it passes. We stay in it.

We also hold each other. Not with judgment, but with honesty. If something lands badly, we say so. If someone's pattern is affecting the group, the group names it. Gently. Directly. Without drama.

## Why this matters

A group without accountability becomes a place where feelings are managed rather than faced. Where the same story gets told the same way every week and nothing shifts. Accountability is what keeps the work alive.

The sources call it different things. AA calls it taking a fearless moral inventory. The Mankind Project calls it owning your shadow. Bill Kauth calls it responsibility. It's the same thing: you can't change what you won't acknowledge.
