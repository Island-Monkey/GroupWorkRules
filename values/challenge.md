# Challenge

> This is not a comfort group. Comfort is easy. Growth costs something.

## For our group this means

We are here to be moved, not just heard. A good men's group does both: it holds you, and it pushes back. The holding without the push is just sympathy. The push without the holding is just confrontation. Together they're something else.

We offer challenge with care. Not to score points, not to fix, not to prove how clear-sighted we are. We challenge because we believe the person in front of us is capable of more than the story they're currently telling about themselves.

We stay open to being challenged in return. That's the deal.

## What this looks like

Asking the question that nobody else is asking. Noticing when someone's been telling the same story for three months. Sitting with someone in discomfort rather than rushing to resolve it. Not nodding along when nodding along isn't honest.

## Rooted in the sources

The Mankind Project builds challenge into its core model: the group is a place where men are held accountable to their potential, not just their pain. Bill Kauth's framework names challenge as one of the pillars alongside support. The distinction matters: support groups support. Men's groups support *and* challenge.

The two together are what make change possible.
